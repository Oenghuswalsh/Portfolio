About me

The Developer

I am a dedicated and passionate individual with a keen interest in the world of web development. As a recent graduate with a Certificate IV in Information Technology (Website Development) from Lumify, I have set my sights on a career as a Front-End Developer. My journey in this field has been driven by an unwavering curiosity and a commitment to continuous learning.


With a solid foundation in HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript, I am enthusiastic about creating visually appealing and responsive web experiences. My quest for knowledge extends beyond the classroom, as evidenced by my pursuit of various LinkedIn certifications, including MySQL, Figma, and Visual Studio Code. These certifications reflect my dedication to mastering the tools and technologies essential to this dynamic industry.


What sets me apart is my diverse background, encompassing roles in project management, design, and teamwork. My experiences as a Kitchen Planner/Designer at IKEA, a business owner with "Designed in Wood," and various roles in the furniture industry have equipped me with valuable transferable skills. I bring a unique blend of project management, attention to detail, and effective communication to the world of web development.


I am drawn to the limitless possibilities of web development and the opportunity to craft outstanding user experiences. With a keen eye for detail and a penchant for solving complex challenges, I aspire to contribute to projects that leave a lasting impact. My mission is to continuously enhance my skills, collaborate with like-minded professionals, and bring my creativity to the forefront of web development. If you're seeking a motivated and adaptable Front-End Developer, I'm eager to connect and explore how we can shape the digital world together.


The Person

I have always been creative and in my working life I have had the opportunity to work in many creative ways. As a furniture designer/maker for 25 plus years I have been designing, developing and have created many things. Leaving school I started a cabinet making apprenticeship in a handmade furniture business and from there moved into more automated manufacturing processes. This is where I started operating CNC machines and learned to program and design in 3D softwares and cad cam programs. I found a drive and excitement within myself to create processes that were more efficient and would push the boundaries of the machine and systems, and sometimes these ideas did not work or I learned that to keep all the teams working, keeping it simple is sometimes better. But most importantly I had found something I was passionate about doing.


I had knowledge of materials, products, machines and the software, teams and people and with these abilities I worked on my skills in project management and communicating with other departments and clients. This allowed me to move into more design and project related roles where I had more direct contact with clients and was fully responsible for the project design and development for production. Here I developed skills in client relationships and inter departmental communication as well as gaining experience in project management. This eventually led me to start my own business as a furniture designer/maker, which presented its own challenges like business management, business finances, customer prospecting, customer retention and many more.


After 5 successful years I put this business aside as we relocated our family and moving the business was not possible so I became a kitchen sales and designer with IKEA, very quickly I mastered this role which allowed me to share my knowledge of kitchen design and manufacturing with my colleagues and customers alike. I found great pride in educating customers and watching them walk away knowing they can make a better decision even if I didn't make that sale!


After another family relocation, this time from Ireland to Australia, I suddenly had lots of free time so I entered into the world of tech and web development. All of my experiences up to this point allowed me to transition into coding quite easily. I also realized I had a passion for coding too as I could be just as creative as I used to be in furniture making. And many of the processes I used were perfect for web development which has given me a great foundation to start from, now I just need to learn the coding languages!


I started to dabble in web development and coding and then did some online courses and eventually a certificate course in IT and web development which I successfully graduated, and now I am working on some projects to create a portfolio of work to showcase myself and hopefully inspire you to get in touch if even just for a chat over coffee!
